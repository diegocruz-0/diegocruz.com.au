About me

Who I am

Building things that actually work.

I'm Diego — a Cloud Engineer based in Sydney with over eight years in enterprise IT infrastructure. I work at Ingram Micro, where I help businesses across Australia and New Zealand design, migrate, and optimise their Microsoft cloud environments.

My technical background covers Windows Server, networking, virtualisation, identity, and Azure — and I hold the AZ-104 Microsoft Azure Administrator Associate certification, with more on the way. I care about building systems that are reliable, secure, and built to scale.

But the reason I'm good at my job has less to do with certifications and more to do with a mindset I've cultivated over years of cooking, training, and tinkering: patience, precision, and a genuine love for understanding how things work.

"The best systems — in technology, in cooking, in life — are the ones you understand deeply enough to trust."

Outside of work, I spend my time on the BJJ mat, by the Kamado Joe, and generally trying to get better at the things I care about. I'm based in Sydney, originally from Brazil, and have spent my career building technical expertise in the Microsoft ecosystem. Fernando the cat tolerates all of it.

How I think

Principles I live by.

Discipline

Show up, every day

BJJ teaches this clearly: talent is outpaced by consistency. The same applies to engineering, cooking, and any craft worth pursuing. The best results come from showing up repeatedly, not from occasional bursts of effort.

Curiosity

Ask why, then go deeper

I don't like using things I don't understand. That drives me to learn infrastructure from first principles, to read the docs, to experiment in the lab. Understanding the why makes you better at the what.

Craftsmanship

Details are the design

Whether I'm smoking a brisket for 12 hours or designing a network architecture, the quality is in the details most people skip. Good enough is rarely good enough for me — and that's something I'm proud of.
